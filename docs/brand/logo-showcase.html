The Stamp — A Logo System

One idea, four stamps.

ZeroSpam's logo is the wordmark, the wordmark is the brand, and the brand is the yellow dot. The four variants below are the same idea at different sizes — italic Source Serif and that single dot, scaled and cut to fit. Don't add a fifth.

01 — The Wordmark primary · horizontal

ZeroSpam

The master mark. Source Serif 4 Italic, weight 500. Yellow dot punctuates the brand in place of a U+00B7 — but rendered as a circle, not a glyph, so it stays perfectly round at any scale. Use it horizontally with at least 18px of cap height.

02 — The Monogram tight horizontal · cli

z

For places where the full wordmark won't fit. Lowercase italic z reads as "z[ero]." — the period is the dot. Always lowercase, always italic. Use it in a sidebar collapse state, the CLI splash, an email signature footer.

03 — The Seal brand stamp · app icon

The cut-out seal.

A solid ink disc with the lowercase italic z cut through to whatever surface is behind. The yellow dot lives inside the disc, anchored lower-right. On cream paper the z reads cream. On dark surfaces, swap to the reversed seal.

04 — The Favicon browser chrome · 16-32 px

The pixel-tuned variant.

At favicon scale, the lowercase z's descender curl turns to mush. We swap to uppercase italic Z at weight 600 — heavier strokes, cleaner read. Rounded square instead of circle so browser chrome doesn't crop it to nothing.
